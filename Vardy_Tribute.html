The story of Jamie Vardy's Football Career

  • 1987 - Born in Sheffield, England
  • 2003 - Released from the Sheffield Wednesday youth academy
  • 2007 - Joins the Stocksbridge Park Steels, making £30 per week
  • 2010 - FC Halifax Town pays a £15,000 fee for the striker's talents
  • 2011 - Signed for Fleetwood Town FC in the Conference Premier League, one level below the Football League
  • 2012 - After leading the league in scoring, Leicester City FC signed Vardy from Fleetwood Town for a non-league record of £1m
  • 2014 - Leicester City gets promoted to the Premier League after Vardy scores 16 league goals and is named Player's Player of the Season
  • 2015 - Vardy plays a crucial role in the Foxes' "Great Escape" that saves them from relegation back to the Championship
  • 2015 - After a hot start to the season, Vardy breaks Ruud Van Nistelroy's record for consecutive Premier League matches with a goal (pictured above)
  • 2016 - The partnership of Vardy and Riyad Mahrez up front propels Leicester City to a miracle title that captivates the entire football community
  • 2016 - With 24 goals in the 2015-16 season, Vardy was named to the Team of the Year, alongside four other Leicester players
  • 2016 - UEFA Euro 2016 appearance for England
  • 2018 - Travels to Russia for the 2018 FIFA World Cup
  • 2020 - After more prolific seasons, Vardy scores his 100th Premier League goal and is named the Golden Boot winner for the 2019-20 season
  • 2021 - Vardy continues to get on the scoresheet, finishing with 15 goals in the Premier League. He also helped lead the foxes to their first ever FA Cup trophy
